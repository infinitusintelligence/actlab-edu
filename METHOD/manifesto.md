We believe that **technological education** is a fundamental process for preparing people for today‚Äôs world ‚Äî a world defined by constant change, complexity, and innovation.

**ACT LAB** is a **support process for formal education**, designed to strengthen technical learning through action, creation, and student agency. It does not replace formal education ‚Äî it **enhances and complements it**.

In ACT LAB, students move beyond passive content consumption and become **protagonists and owners of their ideas**. Learning happens through doing, deciding, experimenting, and taking responsibility for what is built.

Projects are not isolated tasks.  
They are **spaces for engagement, authorship, and meaning**.

Autonomous, guided, and responsible learning enables students to understand how they can **intervene in reality through technology**, turning problems into concrete and relevant solutions. In this way, education becomes directly connected to the real world.

Engagement is not enforced.  
It emerges when students recognize purpose, value, and impact in their learning process.

Teachers play a central role.  
In ACT LAB, teachers act as **mediators and mentors**, structuring the learning environment, guiding technical decisions, encouraging reflection, and ensuring academic rigor.

This mediation transforms action into learning and experience into structured knowledge.

ACT LAB exists to **prepare people for the contemporary world**, fostering technical competence, intellectual autonomy, and responsibility for decisions and outcomes.

**ACT LAB is technological education with student agency.  
It is action that leads to creation.  
It is learning that transforms realities.**
