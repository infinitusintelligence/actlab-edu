## 1. ACT LAB Definition

**ACT LAB** is a project-based learning method structured as an **educational project office**, designed to function as a **support process for formal education**, particularly within technical and technological education contexts.

Its purpose is to promote learning through **action**, **creation**, and **student agency**, preparing learners for the challenges of the contemporary world.

---

## 2. Purpose of the Method

ACT LAB aims to:

- strengthen technical education through integrated, practical experiences;
- develop autonomy, responsibility, and intellectual authorship;
- bridge education and real professional contexts;
- prepare students to operate in environments of innovation, technology, and constant change.

The method does **not replace formal curricula**, but **enhances and integrates** them.

---

## 3. Pedagogical Foundations

ACT LAB is grounded in:

- Project-Based Learning (PBL);
- Applied technological education;
- Student agency and protagonism;
- Curricular integration;
- Formative and process-based assessment.

These foundations guide all methodological decisions.

---

## 4. Organizational Structure

### 4.1 Educational Project Office

ACT LAB operates as an **educational project office**, simulating professional environments of technology development and innovation.

### 4.2 Student Organization

- Projects developed **in pairs**;
- Shared responsibility;
- Guided autonomy;
- Progressive deliverables over time.

---

## 5. Roles in ACT LAB

### Students
- act as protagonists of the learning process;
- make technical and organizational decisions;
- develop, test, and present solutions;
- take responsibility for their choices.

### Teacher
The teacher acts as a **mediator and mentor**, responsible for:

- structuring the learning environment;
- guiding technical and methodological decisions;
- ensuring academic rigor;
- monitoring project development;
- fostering critical reflection.

---

## 6. Methodological Cycle

ACT LAB operates through a continuous cycle composed of:

1. Contextualization  
2. Ideation  
3. Planning  
4. Development  
5. Testing and validation  
6. Documentation  
7. Presentation and reflection  

The cycle is iterative and adaptable to context and learner pace.

---

## 7. Curricular Integration

ACT LAB integrates content from multiple disciplines, including:

- Programming;
- Databases;
- Networks and infrastructure;
- Hardware;
- Design;
- Software engineering.

This integration enables meaningful and applied learning.

---

## 8. Assessment

Assessment in ACT LAB is:

- continuous;
- process-oriented;
- formative;
- integrated into project development.

Technical, organizational, collaborative, and reflective aspects are considered.

---

## 9. Scalability and Adaptation

ACT LAB is a method that is:

- replicable;
- adaptable to different educational contexts;
- scalable across levels and areas.

It can be applied in technical programs, integrated high schools, institutional projects, and teacher training initiatives.

---

---

## 11. Final Statement

ACT LAB proposes a form of technological education that prepares individuals to **act**, **create**, and **take responsibility** for their learning and decisions.
