## üá∫üá∏ ENGLISH

The principles of ACT LAB guide all pedagogical, methodological, and organizational decisions within the method. They ensure coherence between practice, educational intent, and learning outcomes.

---

### 1. Student Agency

Students are the central agents of the learning process.  
They make decisions, build solutions, and take responsibility for their choices and deliverables.

ACT LAB does not operate through mechanical task execution, but through **intellectual authorship and active engagement**.

---

### 2. Learning Through Action

In ACT LAB, learning means **acting**.  
Knowledge emerges from practice, experimentation, and real problem-solving.

Action is the starting point for technical and conceptual learning.

---

### 3. Purposeful Creation

Creation in ACT LAB is intentional.  
It is connected to real problems, contexts, and needs.

To create is to understand the impact of what is built and to take responsibility for the outcome.

---

### 4. Applied Technological Education

Technology is treated as a **means to transform reality**, not as an end in itself.

ACT LAB integrates theory and practice to form students capable of understanding, building, and applying technology consciously.

---

### 5. Autonomy with Responsibility

The method promotes guided autonomy.  
Freedom of choice exists within clear criteria, defined timelines, and assumed responsibilities.

Autonomy is formative and progressive.

---

### 6. Teacher as Mediator and Mentor

The teacher acts as a **mediator of the learning environment** and a **mentor of the formative process**.

The teacher guides, encourages reflection, ensures technical rigor, and supports student development without replacing student action.

---

### 7. Meaningful Learning

Learning occurs when students recognize meaning in what they do.

ACT LAB connects content, projects, and real-world contexts, strengthening engagement and knowledge retention.

---

### 8. Continuous and Reflective Process

Learning is continuous.  
Error is understood as part of development.

Reflection on decisions, outcomes, and paths taken is a central element of the method.

---

### 9. Curricular Integration

ACT LAB integrates knowledge from different technical and conceptual domains.

This integration fosters a systemic, applied, and contextualized understanding of learning.

---

### 10. Preparation for the Contemporary World

ACT LAB prepares students for a world in constant transformation.

Beyond teaching specific technologies, the method develops the **ability to learn, adapt, and create**.
