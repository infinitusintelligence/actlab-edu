## üá∫üá∏ ENGLISH

The ACT LAB methodological cycle structures project development over time, ensuring progression, rigor, and practical meaning.  
Each stage prepares students to **present their projects to external audiences**, including entrepreneurs, supporters, institutions, and ecosystem partners.

---

## Cycle Overview

ACT LAB operates through a **continuous, iterative, delivery-oriented cycle**, designed to simulate real-world technology project environments.

The cycle culminates in an **external presentation**, where students communicate their solutions in a clear, technical, and strategic manner.

---

## Cycle Stages

### 1. Contextualization

Students are introduced to:
- real or simulated challenges;
- social, technological, or productive contexts;
- demands from local or global innovation ecosystems.

Goal: understand the problem and its impact.

---

### 2. Ideation

Students:
- propose ideas and possible solutions;
- explore technical alternatives;
- assess initial feasibility.

Goal: turn problems into proposals.

---

### 3. Planning

Student pairs define:
- project scope;
- technologies and tools;
- execution timeline;
- responsibilities.

Goal: structure action.

---

### 4. Development

Projects are built incrementally through:
- technical implementation;
- continuous testing;
- adjustments and refinements.

Goal: turn plans into functional products.

---

### 5. Testing and Validation

Students:
- test functionalities;
- validate assumptions;
- assess performance and usability.

Goal: ensure solution quality and coherence.

---

### 6. Documentation

Projects are documented professionally, including:
- problem and solution descriptions;
- technical decisions;
- lessons learned and limitations.

Goal: communicate the project professionally.

---

### 7. Preparation for External Presentation

Students prepare:
- technical presentations;
- project narratives;
- demonstrations and prototypes;
- responses to external questions.

Goal: prepare communication with the real world.

---

### 8. External Presentation

Projects are presented to:
- entrepreneurs;
- supporters;
- institutional partners;
- potential funders.

Goal: expose student work to external evaluation, expand perspective, and strengthen student agency.

---

### 9. Reflection and Iteration

After the presentation, students:
- reflect on received feedback;
- evaluate decisions and outcomes;
- refine or evolve the project.

Goal: consolidate learning and critical thinking.

---

## Teacher‚Äôs Role in the Cycle

The teacher acts as a **mediator and mentor**, ensuring:
- pedagogical coherence;
- technical rigor;
- ethical and formative alignment;
- support in preparing external presentations.

---

## Expected Outcome

At the end of the cycle, students:
- deliver a functional project;
- develop technical communication skills;
- interact with external innovation ecosystems;
- expand their understanding of entrepreneurship, innovation, and impact.
