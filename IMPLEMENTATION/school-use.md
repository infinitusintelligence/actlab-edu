## 1. Purpose of School Use

**ACT LAB** is an educational method designed to function as a **support process for formal education**, particularly within technical education and integrated high school programs.

Its use in schools aims to:
- strengthen technical learning;
- integrate curricular content;
- promote student agency;
- connect education with real-world and innovation ecosystems.

ACT LAB **does not replace subjects, content, or mandatory workloads**. It operates in a **complementary and integrative** manner.

---

## 2. Institutional Framework

ACT LAB may be implemented:
- as an integrative axis of technical programs;
- in association with existing technical subjects;
- as a continuous project throughout the academic year;
- aligned with the school‚Äôs Political-Pedagogical Project (PPP).

The method is compatible with **educational regulations, BNCC, and vocational education requirements**.

---

## 3. School Time Organization

ACT LAB implementation respects:
- the official curriculum structure;
- subject workloads;
- the official academic calendar.

Projects are developed throughout the year, reducing fragmentation and enabling deeper learning.

---

## 4. Student Role in School Use

Within the school context, students:
- actively participate in project development;
- apply classroom knowledge;
- take responsibility for decisions and deliverables;
- develop progressive autonomy.

Students are assessed based on both process and outcomes.

---

## 5. Teacher Role in School Use

In ACT LAB, the teacher:
- acts as a mediator of the learning process;
- guides technical and methodological decisions;
- ensures pedagogical and ethical rigor;
- monitors project development;
- articulates curricular content with project activities.

The teacher remains the **central pedagogical authority**.

---

## 6. Integration with School Assessment

Assessment in ACT LAB:
- is continuous and process-based;
- may be integrated into subject assessments;
- considers technical, organizational, and collaborative aspects;
- generates auditable pedagogical evidence.

---

## 7. Adaptation to Different Contexts

ACT LAB is adaptable to:
- public and private schools;
- diverse territorial realities;
- different student profiles;
- varying infrastructure levels.

The method prioritizes pedagogical coherence and institutional feasibility.

---

## 8. Limits of School Use

ACT LAB:
- is not an isolated extracurricular activity;
- does not replace the formal curriculum;
- does not eliminate the need for instructional classes when appropriate.

It functions as an **organizing structure for pedagogical practice**, not as an end in itself.

---
